Prompt: Analyze this document

Act as a senior analyst and walk me through everything I need to understand the shared document. It could be a speech, article, research paper, blog post, memo, report, or something else entirely. 

Cover the following:

What is this? Identify the type of document, when it was created, who wrote it, and for what audience. Summarize the entire thing in 2–3 sentences.

What is it saying? Lay out the core argument, thesis, or message. What claims does the author make? What are the key points, and in what order does the author present them? Distinguish between what is stated explicitly and what is implied.

Why does it exist? What is the author trying to accomplish — inform, persuade, entertain, sell, justify, provoke? What problem, event, or question motivated this document? What context (political, cultural, economic, institutional) is needed to understand why it was written now, by this person, for this audience?

What supports it? Identify the evidence, data, examples, anecdotes, or authorities the author uses. How strong is that evidence? Are sources cited, verifiable, and credible? Where is the reasoning solid?

What weakens it? Identify gaps, unsupported claims, logical fallacies, missing perspectives, contradictions, or unstated assumptions. What questions does the document fail to answer? What counterarguments does the author ignore or dismiss too quickly?

Who benefits and who doesn’t? Consider whose interests are served by this document’s conclusions. Whose voices or perspectives are present? Whose are absent? If the recommendations were followed, who gains and who loses?

How is it constructed? Comment on the structure, tone, language choices, and rhetorical techniques. Is the writing effective for its purpose and audience? Does the style strengthen or undermine the message?

What does it connect to? Place this document in a larger conversation. What other works, events, trends, or debates does it relate to? Does it confirm, challenge, or extend existing thinking on the topic?

What should I take from it? Given everything above, what parts of this document are trustworthy and useful? What parts should I treat with skepticism? What’s the single most important thing a reader should walk away understanding?

What should I do next? Based on this analysis, suggest 3–5 follow-up actions — additional documents to read, questions to research, people or perspectives to seek out, or decisions this document should inform.

Be specific and direct. Use quotes or references from the document to support your analysis. If the document is ambiguous or you’re uncertain about the author’s intent, say so and explain why.
